## The first thing — questions and listening

> Normally - I just need to ask a lot of questions. That's the start. I have a wide range of
> experience and I pick up on systems and goals quickly - but there's no replacement for the
> domain experience and getting a sense of what the challgenges and emotions are so far.
> Sometiems people ahve been thinking about this for a decade or more / and they might be too
> close to it - or they might have just started. Getting to know the people and enough of the
> situation for the next phase really matters. That's kinda tha first thing that has to happen.
> A lot of listeneting, and questions, and notes. Sometimes we'll be able to jump right into an
> excisting interface or they'll have drawinds or a progotype - and someteims I can even build
> something loose while we're talking to help / or point out some exisitng examples we can use
> to explore.

## Divergent / convergent, and trust

> I don't relaly do the "double diamond" - but I think there is huge value in splitting up the
> divergent and convergent thinking. i can't help them decide on something -- if we don't know
> all the options. So, the first area is about building enough trust to get them to invest the
> time in thath divergent thinking. Then I can take that - and narrow things down enough to
> choose the next course of action.

## No formal order — reaching experts and users

> There's no formal order. Many of these phases coul dhappen in different orders. But after I get
> the initial download, I'm very likely going to reach out to some experts. I'm not the worlds
> most connected pers on- but I seem to always be able to reach out to a couple kep people whether
> that's in a domain like realty or investing or even a locl lawyer or judge. I can find a few
> peopel with domain expertise and a few people who would be users of whaetver we're exploring.
> Even just 5 people can yield huge info. And with that -- I can start a more informed research
> phase. That might be the second phase. And it's important because those people will give me very
> differnt leads.

## Known vs unique work — paper testing, engine before polish, founding designer

> There's a certain amount of this work that is known. We might have to choose a font - or a color.
> We're probably going to have some forms. But every project has a set of things that are unique.
> They might be legal challenges, they might be technical. There may be a marketing system that
> needs to get put in place. in many cases -- the unique parts -- the real key values though --
> they can be tested on paper. If somoene can't go through a signup flow on paper - and use a
> checklist -- making a really expensive web application to find out - isn't the way to go. it
> doesn't have to be on paper / I might build it as a throwaway prototype o pass around. I used to
> be one of the only people who could whip up something liek that - but now AI tools make it so
> anyone can. The key is to make sure it's the right thing to make! So many comanies or agencies
> will start with a graphic designer - and on the surface level of things -- but that's the polish
> that only matters if the engine benith it is solid and worth building at all. This is why I'm an
> effective founding designer. I've seen it go all the wrong ways. The graphics will come when it's
> time. This is about breaking the goal up into managable pieces so we can split up the work and
> getting people empowered to focus in their most effective area. It migth be time for us all to go
> create some prototypes in our own domain and get testting. The back end devs might need to test
> out a new tyep of database system - or consider a unique scaling issue. The legal team might need
> to get out ahead of things. We need to assemble the right people to for this next phase to spread
> out and get a temperature reading. This is also a very importatnt time to make sur eeveryone is
> working together and distill our goals down as far as we can. this is not the time for silos -
> and so, while we converge on the moment - it's time to open back pu to the next divergent phase -

## Sending people out — parallel work on a real low-fi foundation

> The next step is really sending the people out to gather enough info that we can make smart
> decisions. So, they'll be out doing that. That might be early user interviews, competitive market
> research, trying some of what we are guessint are going to be the hardest or most expensive parts,
> preppting the system so that everyond can work in paralle. If we know we'll need a db and auth and
> a sysetm of roles and routes -- well, there's no reason we can't have a low fidelity live
> foundatino in place to actually use and be sure. While that's happening it's only natural be
> working out the information artchitecture that drives it and prove it. visual designers can be
> taking what we now know to be mandagory - and start using those data structures to explore visual
> language and build mood boards adn style tiles - while front-end devs can map out the tricker
> dtails of the user interfaces. This all allows UX designers to use the real prototypes and system
> to start exploring and testing with users. Everyone on the team - can use the real things - and
> give feedback the entrire time / no surpreises to waste time explaning with expensive pitch decks
> - because everyone all knows where we're at--- all the time.
